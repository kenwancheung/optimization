Question 2¶

We have four different currencies (EUR, USD, GBP, JPY) and bid/ask spreads involve pairs of the currencies, giving using 4!/(2!*2!) = 6 combinations. Thinking in terms of Linear Programming, we could translate this problem statement and create 4 columns and 2 equations.

USD EUR GBP JPY
USD 1 .87063 1/1.42790 133.333
EUR 1/0.87060 1 0.60972 116.144
GBP 1.42796 1/0.60970 1 190.476
JPY 1/133.330 1/116.14 1/190.480 1

From this table, we can derive 12 variables (USD to EUR, EUR to USD, EUR to GBP, etc). From there, we can set up constraints for each currency. Since we're only dealing in USD, we set our constraints to zero for the other currencies.

Total available dollars = Initial Amount + (EUR --> USD) + (GBP --> USD) + (JPY --> USD)
Total distributed dollars = Final dollar holdings + (USD --> EUR) + (USD --> GBP) + (USD --> JPY)

Objective: Maximize y subject to
USD constraint: y + x12 + x13 + x14 - (1/0.87060*x21 + 1/1.42790*x31 + 1/133.330*x41) = 1
EUR constraint: x21 + x23 + x24 - (.87063*x12 + 1/0.6097*x32 + 1/116.14*x42) = 0
GBP constraint: x31 + x32 + x34 - (1.42796*x13 + 0.60972*x23 + 1/190.480*x43) = 0
JPY constraint: x41 + x42 + x43 - (133.333*x14 + 116.144*x24 + 190.476*x34) = 0

From this, we can form our strategy as follows:

1) Trade your dollar for GBP ... $1 becomes 1.42796 pounds.
2) Trade your pounds for EUR ... 1.42796 GBP becomes 1.42796*0.60972 = 0.8706557712 Euros.
3) Trade your euros for USD ... 0.8706557712 Euros becomes 1.00006406065 dollars.
4) Repeat thousands of times until you have 10,000 USD!
